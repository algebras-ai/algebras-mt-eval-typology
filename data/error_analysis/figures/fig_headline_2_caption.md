Figure 2: The calibration–variance trade-off across the four
(mode × judge) combinations. Fluency-MQM variants achieve higher slope
against human MQM (0.515–0.552) at the cost of reduced variance
(std 9.66–15.91 on a 0–100 scale), due to saturation near 100.
Fluency2 positive variants retain wider variance (std 17.03–18.32)
but track human score less tightly (slope 0.266–0.282). The two
modes are structurally complementary rather than substitutable.
